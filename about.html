What do I bring to the table as a developer?

I’m a web developer with professional experience in product management and interface positions. I know how to organize myself within the time constraints of a project and align interests with colleagues from different departments. My experience as a branding and naming specialist has also taught me the importance of incorporating the customer perspective into the product.

Having spent two years alone in China - the first time at 16 - has taught me to self-reliantly find solutions and continue trying when encountering an obstacle. I did not give up easily when I subsequently studied business Chinese, in contrary it coined my drive and attention to detail.

Now, as a developer, when doing a project, I do not shy away when an application suddenly throws errors but always strive to find solutions - with the help of documentation, forums, tutorials, by rolling back or simply asking fellow developers. I believe in life-long-learning and am always willing to expand my horizon and learn something completely new. Also, my attention to detail comes in handy when finding bugs and doing quality assurance.

What I am looking for?

I’m looking to collaborate on projects with a good cause. If it is outside my current scope, the better. I am willing to study intensely to learn.

My professional goal is to find a full-time position at a remote-first company that works with an agile mindset and supports structured process retrospectives to learn and improve continuously. I am looking for a role in a positive team, where we can share knowledge to build visually appealing and user-focused web applications.
